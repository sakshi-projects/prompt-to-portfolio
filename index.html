CHRIS COLUMBUS

Mastermind Behind Harry Potter's Magical Beginning

The Visionary

Chris Columbus is a cornerstone of modern American cinema, renowned for crafting stories that define childhood for generations. From penning 80s classics like The Goonies to directing Home Alone, he has mastered the art of family wonder.

His greatest legacy remains the cinematic foundation of the Wizarding World. By directing the first two Harry Potter films, Columbus established the visual language and iconic casting that allowed the franchise to become a global phenomenon.

Iconic Directorial Works

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (2001)

The film that launched a global phenomenon—$975M+ worldwide.

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (2001)

Chris Columbus brought J.K. Rowling's magical world to life with young Daniel Radcliffe, Rupert Grint, and Emma Watson. The film introduced Hogwarts, captured the wonder of childhood magic, and became the highest-grossing film of 2001 with over $975 million worldwide.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002)

Darker magic, deeper mystery—$879M worldwide triumph.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002)

Columbus returned for darker adventures with the mysterious Chamber of Secrets. Featuring stunning effects like the Basilisk and Dobby's debut, it earned $879 million while deepening the Harry Potter mythology with themes of prejudice and hidden dangers.

Home Alone (1990)

Holiday classic that became the top live-action comedy ($476M).

Home Alone (1990)

Macaulay Culkin's Kevin McCallister defends his home with ingenious traps against burglars. This holiday classic became the highest-grossing live-action comedy ever at $476M and defined Christmas movie traditions worldwide.

Home Alone 2: Lost in New York (1992)

Big Apple chaos sequel ($359M success).

Home Alone 2: Lost in New York (1992)

Kevin's New York adventure features iconic Plaza Hotel scenes and even bigger traps. The sequel grossed $359M and became a holiday staple with Tim Curry and Brenda Fricker adding memorable villainy.

Mrs. Doubtfire (1993)

Robin Williams masterpiece ($441M worldwide).

Mrs. Doubtfire (1993)

Robin Williams' Oscar-nominated performance as a divorced dad in nanny disguise. This heartfelt family comedy grossed $441M and remains one of the highest-grossing comedies of the 1990s.

Percy Jackson & the Olympians: The Lightning Thief (2010)

Mythic quest adaptation ($226M).

Percy Jackson & the Olympians: The Lightning Thief (2010)

Logan Lerman stars as the demigod discovering his heritage in Rick Riordan's mythic world. Uma Thurman as Medusa and Pierce Brosnan as a centaur brought Greek mythology to modern audiences with $226M box office.
